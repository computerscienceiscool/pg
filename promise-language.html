Promise language

A compact vocabulary reference for PromiseGrid writing. These are the words the Promise Lens rules flag, the softer or more precise replacements they suggest, and the places where a flagged term is still acceptable.

Source: this page is derived from the PromiseLens repo, https://github.com/ciwg/promise-lens. That repo contains both a Chrome extension and a CLI for flagging language that drifts away from Promise Theory framing. The authored source and runtime rule files currently agree, so this page presents one merged rule list for human review.

How to use it

Flagged words are not automatically forbidden. They are hotspots for review. Keep them when the page is intentionally contrasting centralized or punitive framing, or when a technical exception is necessary for precision.

Promise Chain means linked promises across time Prefer promises, offers, acceptance, local assessment, trust, credibility Use exceptions deliberately, not by habit Treat the rule list as editorial guidance, not blind search-replace

Built-in exceptions

Some rules already ship with explicit exceptions. Those should not be flagged by the scanner, and they are safe to keep when they mean exactly what they say.

`control`

`version control`, `access control`, `control loop`, `control-loop`

`proof`

`proof-of-concept`, `future-proof`

Contrast note

`authority` and `central authority` may still be useful when the page is explicitly naming centralized models as a contrast or risk.
